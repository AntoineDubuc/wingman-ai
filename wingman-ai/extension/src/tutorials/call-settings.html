Call Settings

Configure transcription sensitivity, suggestion frequency, speaker filter, and call summaries

1
Open Call Settings

Go to Settings and click the Call Settings tab. Here you can fine-tune how Wingman transcribes audio and generates suggestions during your calls.

2
Adjust Endpointing

Endpointing controls how long Wingman waits after speech stops before finalizing a transcript. Lower values (400ms) create faster, choppier transcripts. Higher values (1000ms) wait longer for complete sentences.

Recommended: 700ms (default) works well for most conversations. Lower it for fast-paced calls, raise it if transcripts are cutting off mid-sentence.
3
Configure Speaker Filter

Speaker Filter lets you choose which audio sources to transcribe: your microphone, the meeting audio (other participants), or both. This helps focus suggestions on what matters most.

Use cases: "Both" for full context. "Meeting only" to focus on what prospects say. "Mic only" if you want suggestions based on your own speech patterns.
4
Suggestion Cooldown

The suggestion cooldown prevents Wingman from overwhelming you with suggestions. Default is 15 seconds between suggestions. Adjust based on your call pace and API limits.

API limits: Lower cooldowns use more API quota. If you're on a free tier, consider keeping this at 15+ seconds.
5
Call Summary Options

Enable automatic call summaries to get a structured recap when your call ends. You can also enable auto-save to Google Drive to keep a record of all your calls.
