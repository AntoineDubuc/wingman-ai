API Keys & Provider Setup

Configure your API keys and choose an AI provider for suggestions

1
Open the Setup Tab

Open the Wingman extension settings. The Setup tab shows your API key configuration and provider selection. You'll need a Deepgram key for transcription and a provider key for AI suggestions.

BYOK: Wingman uses Bring Your Own Keys — no backend server required. Your keys are stored locally in your browser and never sent anywhere except the provider APIs.
2
Get a Deepgram API Key

Deepgram powers real-time speech-to-text transcription. Sign up at console.deepgram.com, create a new API key, and paste it in the Deepgram field.

Free tier: Deepgram offers $200 in free credit when you sign up — enough for many hours of transcription.
3
Choose Your AI Provider

Select which AI provider powers your suggestions. Gemini is recommended for most users (free tier available). Groq offers faster responses with open-source models. OpenRouter gives access to many models including Claude and GPT-4.

Provider comparison: Gemini — best balance of quality and cost. Groq — fastest responses (~100ms). OpenRouter — widest model selection.
4
Configure Your Provider Key

Get an API key from your chosen provider and paste it in the appropriate field. For Groq, visit console.groq.com. For OpenRouter, visit openrouter.ai.

5
Verify Configuration

Once configured, click the Wingman icon in your toolbar. You should see green checkmarks next to "API Keys" indicating everything is ready. Join a Google Meet call to test your setup.
